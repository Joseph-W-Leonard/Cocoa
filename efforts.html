International Conventions

Several international conventions aim to eliminate child labor, including the International Labour Organization (ILO) conventions and the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child. These conventions set standards for the protection of children and the elimination of child labor.

National Efforts

Countries like Brazil, Côte d’Ivoire, and Ghana have implemented national plans and policies to combat child labor. Brazil, for example, has conducted numerous operations to remove children from labor and provide them with education and support. In 2022, Brazil conducted 1,368 operations, removing 2,317 children from child labor.

Corporate Initiatives

Many companies in the cocoa industry have committed to eliminating child labor from their supply chains. Initiatives such as the Child Labor Monitoring and Remediation System (CLMRS) are being implemented to identify and address cases of child labor. Companies like The Hershey Company and Mars, Inc. are actively working to ensure their cocoa is sourced ethically.

Non-Governmental Organizations (NGOs)

NGOs play a crucial role in combating child labor. Organizations like the International Cocoa Initiative (ICI) and the Food Empowerment Project work to raise awareness, provide support to affected children, and advocate for stronger regulations and enforcement.

Community-Based Programs

Community-based programs are essential in addressing the root causes of child labor. These programs focus on improving access to education, providing economic support to families, and raising awareness about the importance of children's rights. By empowering communities, these programs help create sustainable solutions to eliminate child labor.
